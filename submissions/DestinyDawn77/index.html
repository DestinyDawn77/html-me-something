My Journey Begins in Elementary School (1980's)

In the early 1980's, around 2nd grade for me, I was first introduced to the Apple II GE computer. For a second grader, I was amazed and excited! Over the course of the next 4 years, my family acquired a Commodore 64. I immediately started to play around with some basic "coding" on both systems, and the 6th grade, was proud to present my first programs. On the Apple, I had created a visual program, which dispayed a house with moving clouds and a moving smoke stream coming from the chimney. Elementary I know, but for an elementary school student, this was a BIG accomplishment! On the Commodore, I managed to create a very simple game of "Pong", a game involving one paddle at the bottom of the screen, which bounced a small ball around.

My First Programming Class!

My Senior Year of Highschool, I had decided upon the Engineering track, and started courses at our local Community College, as part of a dual-enrollment program. My first class was in FORTRAN. At this time, compilers should have been a four-letter word! We wrote our code on lined paper, submitted the code to our professor for some basic structural review, and finally entered up to 100 lines of code into a compiler... hoping and PRAYING it would work. Nine times out of ten, we received the dreaded "compilation failed" message. There was no "looking for a semi-colon here" or even a "Hey! Your error is on this line!", simply an error message. 100 lines of code and a simple missing semi-colon could be the culprit! As much as I enjoyed the logic and the assembly of programming, those people were CRAZY! I chose to stick with my logic puzzles instead.

But I want to MAKE stuff!!!

Looking back on my early years of college, I only wish someone had mentioned the words "Computer Engineering". WIthin a few years, I had decided Electrical Engineers didn't make the stuff I wanted to make. Circuits? Wonderful! Dynamics, properties of every man-made substance? Not my cup of tea. Disheartened by the lack of "Making Stuff", my degree changed to Graphic Design. Why Graphic Design? I got to MAKE STUFF on the computer! Unfortunately, the Graphic Design industry took a massive downturn about 5 years out of college, following 9-11.

Embrace your Inner-NERD!

The one thing I remind my children of regularly is that "nerds rule the world!". YES! I am a nerd! It took me until my mid thirties to finally admit it. In Highschool, I did everything I could to hide who I really was. Why? Being a nerd wasn't cool. It took a silly, highly addictive video game to bring me out of my shell. About 6 years ago, I stumbled upon a game advertised on facebook called "Lord of Altima". This low-graphics, but highly strategic game attracts nerds of all kinds. I quickly developed friendships with many IT professionals, programmers, web developers, etc. As I heard countless tales of their professional lives, I realized more and more that my continued love for computers and specifically programming could atually turn into a rewarding career! With their encouragement, I returned to school to seek a degree in Computer Engineering. One of my "gaming friends" even offered to mentor me, which he has done for years now. I am 20 credits away from my Associate of Arts degree, with plans to transfer to Washington University to persue my Bachelor's.

One morning in early May, as I was preparing lunches for the kids, I heard a quick mention on the news about this company called "LaunchCode Saint Louis" and their "Summer of Code" program. I dropped the utensiles and came SCREAMING into the living room. I couldn't believe what I was hearing! A program teaching JUST code? Job Placement? No WAY! And here I AM!
